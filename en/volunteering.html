Volunteering

ESN Porto

In September 2015 I started my Erasmus+ Studies mobility period in Gent, Belgium. These were the 5 months that most shifted my views of life. I was in daily contact with people from all over the world, with different life experiences and different ways of thinking. I grew as a person and as a citizen of the world. Back in my hometown, in Porto, I wanted to keep this multicultural spirit alive, so I joined the Erasmus Student Network Porto.

ESN Porto is a no-profit, student run, volunteering association, whose goal is to receive the thousands of international students that every year choose Porto as their mobility destination. We organize several cultural activities, trips all over the country and parties for more than 3000 thousand people a year. However, it's not just fun and games. With ESN Porto I also organized and took part in several social activities: support to the homeless, support to street animals, visits to a day care center ...

This is an association, that despite being student run, is very well structured, where everyone knows its role. Ever since I became full member, I've already took several positions in the structure of ESN Porto:

International Projects Department Coordinator
August 2016 - July de 2017

I coordinated a team of 10 people to implement international ESN projects in the city of Porto. The department organizes several weeks related to each of these projects (involving social inclusion, international mobility, environment protection...). We organize several activities and manage the volunteering of international students in local partner NGOs.

Winner of the award for best International Projects Coordinator of ESN Portugal in 2017
Education Secretary Officer
August 2016 - July 2017

Establishing intitutional relations with other student associations from Porto.

ESNbuddy coordinator in Porto
August de 2017 -

The ESNbuddy program pairs international students with local portuguese students. The latter are responsible for their first contact with portuguese culture and the portuguese people. They will help them navigate through the first weeks of mobility. Organizing and teaching in training sessions for portuguese students, coordinate a team, using web platforms to speed up the process.

Over a 1000 international students assigned. 7 training sessions organized.
IT department coordinator
January 2018 -

Maintain and update de association's website(ESNporto.org). Database management; connection between database, website and POS system, tending to office computers, development of backoffice web apps, Google Drive and Suite admin.

During this entire period I developed several skills, the main ones are: communication, team work and leadership, working in multicultural teams, using Cloud software, webdev (PHP and Drupal), negotiation and costumer relations skills.
